Where this data comes from

Live telemetry published by the Department of Irrigation and Drainage (Jabatan Pengairan dan Saliran). This site is not affiliated with JPS and is not an official warning channel — in an emergency, follow JPS and NADMA directly.

Public Infobanjir JPS Malaysia. Water levels and flood thresholds. Takes priority wherever it carries a station, so a reading matches the national portal.
JPS Selangor Infobanjir Selangor stations, and the only source here for cameras, sirens and flood gauges. Supplies the coordinates every pin is drawn at.
JPS Wilayah Persekutuan (SPHTN) Kuala Lumpur and Putrajaya — water level and rainfall stations the Selangor feed does not cover, with their coordinates.

Each station popup names the feed its reading came from. Where the feeds disagree, the newest reading wins, except that Public Infobanjir's figure is preferred outright.

      How it is worked out

      Each river station is weighted by its level divided by its own danger mark, so a small stream near its limit glows as hot as a big river near its limit.

      That is then scaled by how soon it gets there: a station three hours from its danger mark at the rate it is climbing counts as it stands, one an hour out counts about two-thirds more, and one already at the mark counts double.

      Same two facts the alert panel uses, so what glows and what is listed always agree.

      Rainfall, sirens, gauges and offline stations are not included.
