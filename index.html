Klang Valley
Flood Watch

A live map of flood telemetry for Selangor, Kuala Lumpur and Putrajaya — river levels, rainfall, warning sirens and flood-depth gauges, from every station the Department of Irrigation and Drainage publishes, on one map that refreshes itself.

The official portals each hold a piece of it and none of them plot it together, so answering "is the river near me rising" meant reading three sites and a table. This joins them, works out which stations are actually climbing towards their own danger marks, and shows that instead.

Not an official warning channel. This site is not affiliated with JPS. It re-publishes public data and can be wrong, stale or down — in an emergency, follow JPS and NADMA directly.
If you are in danger, call 999, or the flood emergency line for your state: civildefence.gov.my (APM).

Where this data comes from

Public Infobanjir JPS Malaysia. Water levels and flood thresholds. Takes priority wherever it carries a station, so a reading matches the national portal.
JPS Selangor Infobanjir Selangor stations, and the only source here for cameras, sirens and flood gauges. Supplies the coordinates every pin is drawn at.
JPS Wilayah Persekutuan (SPHTN) Kuala Lumpur and Putrajaya — water level and rainfall stations the Selangor feed does not cover, with their coordinates.

Each station popup names the feed its reading came from. Where the feeds disagree, the newest reading wins, except that Public Infobanjir's figure is preferred outright.

        How it is worked out

        Each station scores its reading against its own thresholds, so a small stream at its limit is as hot as a big river at its limit. Rivers and flood gauges both count — whichever is further up its scale wins the spot.

        The colour is the band it has crossed: yellow past its alert mark, orange past warning, red at danger. The same three marks the station's own meter draws.

        A flood gauge that has tripped goes straight to red — water standing over a spot known to flood is something observed, not forecast.

        Below the alert mark, nothing is drawn. A map that is warm everywhere says nothing.

        Rainfall, sirens and offline stations are not included.
